This page shows some data on the genomic trends of the COVID-19 virus in the UK. Note that all graphs are plotted based on the date the virus sample was taken, and it takes some time for samples to be sequenced. Consequently, data for the last month or so will be based on fewer samples and may not be representative.

This plot shows the prevalence of various mutations which have been suspected to have an effect on the virus's behaviour:

Lineages

This chart shows the prevalence of significant virus lineages (using the cov-lineages.org nomenclature). Sequences not assigned a lineage by COG-UK are not included. Lineages which do not comprise at least 10% of genomes over any 7-day period are recursively merged with their parent lineage.

This graph identifies the prevalence of the variant by nation or by NHS England region of the sequencing centre. English samples sequenced outside England are ignored.
